1. Executive Summary
The Enterprise Data Governance Operations Platform (DGOP) v4.1 represents the evolution of data governance from policy documentation to operational excellence. It is the single operational layer that transforms SDAIA's National Data Index (NDI) from an assessment framework into a living, breathing operational system that runs the enterprise data governance function every day.
DGOP v4.1 is not merely a software application. It is the digital backbone of the enterprise data governance operating model, connecting strategy to execution, accountability to evidence, and compliance to continuous improvement. It serves as the operational engine for the six governance towers: Governance Operations, Compliance Operations, Data Operations, Transparency Operations, Value Operations, and Awareness Operations.
In its v4.1 Enhanced release, DGOP introduces three critical cross-cutting capabilities that elevate the platform from a governance toolset to a unified governance operating environment: (1) Enhanced Search Capabilities — a unified intelligent search that enables users to find any governance object across all modules through a single interface; (2) Error Message Handling & User Notification Framework — a comprehensive communication system ensuring every user interaction produces clear, actionable feedback; and (3) Business Process & Workflow Management — a full BPMN 2.0 workflow engine that operationalizes every governance activity through configurable, automated, auditable workflows. These capabilities, together with the existing seventeen governance modules, establish DGOP as the definitive enterprise data governance platform.
1.1 Vision
To establish DGOP as the definitive operating system for enterprise data governance across the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, enabling every government entity to achieve and sustain NDI 'Leading' maturity through automated, evidence-based, and continuously monitored governance operations.
The vision is anchored in three pillars:
Operationalized Governance: Every governance policy has a corresponding workflow, owner, steward, evidence trail, and KPI in DGOP.
Accountability by Design: Ownership and stewardship are not assumed--they are assigned, approved, tracked, and assessed through the platform.
Evidence-Based Compliance: NDI compliance is not a once-a-year exercise; it is a continuous operational state maintained through automated evidence collection, review, and scoring.
1.2 Strategic Objectives
DGOP v4.1 is designed to achieve the following strategic objectives aligned with Saudi Vision 2030, the National Strategy for Data and AI (NSDA), and the Digital Government Authority (DGA) roadmap:
SO-01: NDI Excellence: Achieve and sustain 'Leading' maturity across all NDI domains (DG, MCM, DQ, DSI, PDP, OD, FOI, DCM, DAM, RMD, BIA, DVR) through automated evidence management and continuous compliance monitoring.
SO-02: Accountability at Scale: Ensure 100% of data assets have certified owners and stewards with defined RACI, effective dates, and performance scorecards.
SO-03: Operational Transparency: Enable real-time visibility into governance health through executive dashboards, domain readiness scores, and operational KPIs.
SO-04: Regulatory Compliance: Enforce PDPL 2023, NCA ECC-1:2024, and CITC cloud compliance through embedded controls, automated workflows, and tamper-evident audit trails.
SO-05: Data Value Realization: Connect governance maturity to business value through data asset valuation, use case governance, and ROI tracking.
SO-06: Workforce Enablement: Build governance capability through tiered training (4 tiers), progressive certification (3 levels), community of practice, and continuous assessment of stewards and owners.
SO-07: Interoperability: Integrate seamlessly with national platforms (Nafath, National Data Platform, GSB, Open Data Portal) and enterprise tools (Catalog, DQ, DLP, ITSM, SIEM, LMS).
SO-08: Continuous Improvement: Implement structured 6-month assessment cycles with gap heat maps, improvement planning, and maturity progression tracking across 4 dimensions.
1.3 Positioning of DGOP
DGOP v4.1 occupies a unique position in the enterprise technology landscape. It is not a data catalog, a data quality tool, a DLP solution, or a metadata repository. It is the governance orchestration layer that sits above these tools and ensures they are operated in a governed, accountable, and compliant manner.
Positioning Statement:
DGOP is the operating system for enterprise data governance. It does not replace your data tools--it governs them.
Key positioning differentiators:
Governance-First Architecture: Built around data domains, subjects, capabilities, and ownership--not just technical metadata.
NDI-Native Design: Every feature maps to an NDI specification, criterion, or maturity level. Compliance is not an afterthought.
Multi-Tower Operations: Supports not just data governance (DG) but also compliance, transparency, value, and awareness operations.
KSA Sovereignty: Designed for CITC-certified cloud deployment with data residency, HSM-backed encryption, and Nafath integration.
Bilingual by Design: Full Arabic and English support with RTL rendering, Hijri date support, and Arabic NLP for FOI classification.
Comprehensive Stewardship Model: Five distinct steward roles (Business, Technical, Operational, Project, Enterprise) with career certification paths.
Organizational Playbook Integration: Embeds charter management, policy lifecycle, decision rights, DGPO sizing, and domain council governance.
Unified Search Capability: Single intelligent search across all governance objects–assets, domains, policies, cases, evidence, stewards, and audit trails–with faceted filters, fuzzy matching, and security-aware results.
Comprehensive Notification Framework: Multi-channel notification system (in-system, email, SMS, push) with configurable preferences, batching, and full audit trail for all user communications.
Business Process & Workflow Management: Full BPMN 2.0 workflow engine with graphical designer, role-based routing, SLA management, approval hierarchies, and complete audit trail for every governance process.

2. Current-State Challenges
Before DGOP v4.1, most KSA government entities operate data governance through a combination of manual processes, disconnected tools, and ad-hoc accountability. The following challenges represent the baseline that DGOP is designed to overcome.
2.1 Fragmented Governance
Governance activities are scattered across multiple departments, tools, and spreadsheets with no unified operational layer:
Data catalogs exist but lack ownership certification, governance status, and NDI mapping.
Data quality tools operate in isolation without clear issue ownership or remediation workflows.
DLP incidents are logged in security tools but never linked to data assets, owners, or governance reviews.
Personal data processing activities are documented in Word documents or Excel sheets with no version control, approval, or audit trail.
Open Data and FOI activities are managed through email, phone calls, and manual logs with no systematic tracking, SLA monitoring, or evidence collection.
NDI evidence is collected annually in a rush, often duplicated, outdated, or disconnected from actual operational activities.
No charter management system: The foundational Data Governance Charter is created once and forgotten, with no versioning, review triggers, or systematic approval workflows.
No policy lifecycle management: Policies are created in an ad-hoc manner with no standardized creation, review, approval, or retirement process across the 5-level hierarchy.
No community platform: Stewards work in silos with no forums, knowledge base, or mentorship program to share best practices.
Impact: Governance is perceived as a bureaucratic overhead rather than an operational enabler. Staff spend more time preparing for assessments than improving data quality or compliance.
2.2 Evidence-Driven Operations Gap
NDI requires evidence of operational maturity. However, most entities cannot produce real-time, continuous evidence of governance activities:
Evidence is created after the fact, often by manually compiling screenshots, emails, and meeting minutes.
There is no chain of custody for evidence--no proof of who created it, when, and whether it was reviewed.
Expired evidence is not automatically flagged; entities discover gaps only during NDI assessments.
Evidence quality is inconsistent--some is strong, some is weak, and there is no standardized assessment rubric.
Gap analysis is manual and subjective. Entities do not have a real-time view of which specifications are met, which are missing, and which are at risk.
Limited maturity dimensions: NDI assessment is evidence-focused only, missing organizational awareness, roles/structures, and value creation dimensions.
Impact: NDI scores are lower than actual maturity because evidence is incomplete, poorly organized, or missing entirely. Entities cannot demonstrate continuous compliance.
2.3 Lack of Accountability
Accountability for data is unclear, contested, or entirely absent across the enterprise:
Data assets are 'owned' by IT departments by default, even when the business content belongs to a line department.
Stewardship is informal--often a single individual who may leave, transfer, or retire without a documented backup.
There is no delegation mechanism. When an owner is unavailable, decisions stall or are made without authority.
Ownership is not linked to job descriptions, performance objectives, or compensation. There is no incentive to steward data well.
Cross-functional data domains (Customer, Citizen, Supplier, Employee) have no single accountable owner because they span multiple departments.
Conflicts over ownership are resolved through politics rather than rules, leading to stalemates and ungoverned assets.
No point-of-entry stewardship: Front-line data entry staff have no defined stewardship role or responsibility for data quality at the point of capture.
No project-specific stewardship: Data governance is absent during project execution (migrations, system implementations), leading to governance gaps.
Impact: Data quality issues persist because no one is accountable for fixing them. Data sharing is delayed because no one has authority to approve. NDI evidence is incomplete because no one is responsible for producing it.
2.4 NDI Pain Points
The SDAIA National Data Index is a powerful framework, but its operationalization creates significant pain points for government entities:

Specification Ambiguity Some specifications are abstract and require interpretation to produce evidence Inconsistent evidence quality across entities and assessors

2.5 Manual Compliance Burden
Compliance with PDPL, NCA ECC, and internal policies is overwhelmingly manual:
PDPL: Data Subject Requests (DSR) are tracked in email inboxes. Consent records are paper-based or scattered. DPIAs are Word documents with no workflow, approval, or version control. Breach notifications are drafted manually and may miss the 72-hour deadline.
NCA ECC: Access reviews are conducted via email and spreadsheet. Privileged access is managed through local admin accounts with no just-in-time elevation. Audit logs are stored in files that are difficult to search or analyze.
Internal Policies: Data retention policies are documented but not enforced. Archival and deletion are manual, error-prone, and rarely verified. Data classification is applied inconsistently across systems. There is no policy lifecycle management system to manage creation, review, approval, and retirement.
Impact: Compliance is expensive, error-prone, and reactive. The risk of regulatory penalties, data breaches, and reputational damage is elevated. Staff morale suffers from repetitive manual work.
DGOP v4.1 is the response to these challenges. It replaces fragmentation with integration, manual effort with automation, ambiguity with clarity, and reactive compliance with continuous operational excellence.
3. Target Operating Model
The DGOP v4.1 Target Operating Model (TOM) redefines enterprise data governance as a set of six interconnected operational towers. Each tower represents a distinct but integrated governance capability that operates continuously through the DGOP platform.
The six towers are:
Governance Operations Tower -- Who owns what, who stewards it, and how accountability is maintained.
Compliance Operations Tower -- How NDI, PDPL, and NCA requirements are operationalized, evidenced, and scored.
Data Operations Tower -- How data quality, metadata, master data, and reference data are governed.
Transparency Operations Tower -- How Open Data and Freedom of Information are managed as governed operational processes.
Value Operations Tower -- How data assets are valued, monetized, and linked to business outcomes.
Awareness Operations Tower -- How the governance workforce is trained, certified, continuously assessed, and supported through a community of practice.
Cross-Tower Enabling Capabilities: Three foundational capabilities underpin and integrate all six towers:
Unified Search (Section 18): A single intelligent search interface that enables users to find any governance object across all towers–assets, cases, policies, evidence, stewards–with faceted filtering, fuzzy matching, and role-based security scope.
Notification Framework (Section 19): A comprehensive multi-channel notification system that ensures every assignment, approval request, reminder, escalation, and system alert reaches the right user through their preferred channel.
Business Process & Workflow Management (Section 20): A full BPMN 2.0 workflow engine that operationalizes governance processes through configurable templates, role-based routing, SLA management, approval hierarchies, and complete audit trails.
These enabling capabilities ensure that governance operations are not isolated within individual towers but are connected, communicated, and process-driven across the entire enterprise.
3.1 Governance Operations Tower
The Governance Operations Tower is the foundational layer of DGOP. It ensures that every data asset, domain, subject, capability, and system has clear, approved, and traceable ownership and stewardship.
Key Functions:
Ownership Assignment Engine: Rule-based, priority-driven assignment of owners and stewards across all dimensions (asset, domain, subject, capability, org unit, system).
Ownership & Stewardship Registry: Official record of all assignments, including effective dates, expiry dates, delegation, backup stewards, and approval history.
5-Role Stewardship Model: Support for Business, Technical, Operational, Project, and Enterprise Data Stewards with distinct responsibilities and certification requirements.
Charter Management: Creation, approval, versioning, and periodic review of the foundational Data Governance Charter with 8 mandatory elements.
Policy Lifecycle Management: Workflow-driven management of the 5-level policy hierarchy (Enterprise -\> Domain -\> Standards -\> Procedures -\> Guidelines).
Conflict Resolution: Automated detection and escalation of ownership conflicts to the Data Governance Committee.
RACI Management: Configurable RACI templates for every governance activity, linked to roles and workflows.
Performance Assessment: Continuous scorecard of steward and owner effectiveness based on SLA compliance, evidence readiness, training completion, certification level, and risk response.
Decision Rights Management: Configurable decision rights matrix with default escalation timeframes (30/14/7/5 days).
Domain Council Governance: Per-domain council management with Lead Steward assignment, meeting cadence, and effectiveness metrics.
Unified Search Capability: Cross-tower intelligent search enabling users to find any governance object–assets, domains, cases, policies, evidence, users–through a single search interface with faceted filters, fuzzy matching, and security-aware result filtering.
Comprehensive Notification Framework: Multi-channel notification system delivering assignment alerts, approval requests, reminders, escalations, and system messages through in-system notifications, email, SMS, and push channels with user-configurable preferences.
Business Process & Workflow Configuration: Graphical workflow designer for modeling governance processes using BPMN 2.0 patterns–sequential, parallel, conditional routing, approval hierarchies, and role-based assignment–with SLA management and complete audit trail.
Operational Outcome: Every data asset has a certified owner and at least one steward. Five distinct steward roles ensure coverage from point-of-entry to executive governance. Ownership gaps are identified in real-time and resolved through workflow-driven escalation. Charter and policy management are systematic and audit-ready. Steward performance is measured and improved continuously. Search, notification, and workflow capabilities operate as enabling services across all six towers, ensuring that every governance activity is discoverable, communicated, and process-driven.
3.2 Compliance Operations Tower
The Compliance Operations Tower transforms regulatory requirements from documents into operational workflows, evidence, and scores.
Key Functions:
NDI Specification Mapping: Hierarchical registry of all NDI domains, criteria, and specifications with acceptance criteria, maturity levels, and evidence requirements.
Evidence Lifecycle: Structured upload, review, approval, expiry, and refresh of evidence with chain-of-custody tracking.
Compliance Scoring: Automated calculation of domain readiness scores based on evidence status, maturity bonuses, and specification weights.
Gap Analysis: Real-time identification of missing, expired, or weak evidence with prioritized remediation queues.
Audit Pack Generation: One-click export of evidence packages, workflow histories, and compliance scores for SDAIA assessors.
PDPL Compliance: Embedded workflows for DSR handling, consent management, RoPA maintenance, DPIA approval, breach notification, and cross-border transfer approval.
NCA ECC Alignment: Control mapping, access review workflows, privileged access management, and SIEM integration for continuous security monitoring.
Operational Outcome: NDI compliance is a continuous operational state, not an annual event. Evidence is always current, reviewed, and audit-ready. PDPL and NCA requirements are enforced through workflows, not manual checks.
3.3 Data Operations Tower
The Data Operations Tower governs the quality, structure, and lifecycle of data assets across the enterprise.
Key Functions:
Data Quality Management: Integration with DQ tools for rule ingestion, issue detection, root cause analysis, remediation assignment, and validation.
6-Dimension DQ Framework: Quality measurement across Accuracy, Completeness, Consistency, Timeliness, Validity, and Uniqueness dimensions.
Data Profiling Integration: Native profiling or adapter-based column analysis, cross-column analysis, and cross-table analysis.
Metadata Management: Synchronization with data catalogs for technical, business, and operational metadata with governance enrichment (owner, steward, classification, NDI mapping).
Master Data Management (MCM): Governance of master data entities, reference data sets, golden records, and data domain hierarchies with matching/survivorship support.
Data Architecture Management (DAM): Conceptual, logical, and physical model governance with architecture review workflows.
Data Lifecycle Management (DCM): Creation, retention, archival, and disposal workflows with automated policy enforcement.
Reference Data Management (RMD): Lifecycle governance of reference data sets including creation, review, approval, and retirement.
Operational Outcome: Data quality is owned, monitored, and improved across 6 dimensions. Metadata is complete, certified, and linked to governance accountability. Master data and reference data are governed as enterprise assets with clear lifecycle management.
3.4 Transparency Operations Tower
The Transparency Operations Tower operationalizes Open Data and Freedom of Information as governed, traceable, and compliant processes.
Key Functions:
Open Data Lifecycle: Candidate identification, eligibility assessment, multi-step approval, automated publication, periodic review, usage monitoring, and retirement.
FOI Lifecycle: Multi-channel intake (email, web, CRM, call center), classification engine, SLA tracking, decision support with templates, appeals management, and evidence archiving.
ODIAO Cockpit: Unified dashboard for the Open Data and Information Access Officer showing backlog, SLA compliance, risks, and workload.
Portal Integration: API-based synchronization with National Open Data Portal and entity portals for publication and usage analytics.
Exemption Management: Rule-based and ML-assisted classification of exemptions (national security, personal data, commercial interests, law enforcement) with public interest override logic.
Operational Outcome: Every Open Data publication and every FOI response is governed by workflow, evidenced for compliance, and monitored for SLA. Transparency is not ad-hoc--it is operationalized.
3.5 Value Operations Tower
The Value Operations Tower connects governance maturity to business value through data asset valuation, use case governance, and ROI tracking.
Key Functions:
Data Asset Valuation: Framework for valuing data assets based on quality, usage, uniqueness, replacement cost, and revenue potential.
Business Impact Assessment (BIA): Governance of data use cases including business case review, data requirements, risk assessment, and value realization tracking.
Data Value Realization (DVR): Tracking of measurable value from data assets including cost savings, revenue generation, decision quality improvement, and operational efficiency.
Data Sharing Value: Measurement of value created through internal and external data sharing including API monetization and partnership revenue.
Operational Outcome: Data governance is justified by measurable business value. Data assets are treated as balance sheet items with documented valuation and ROI. Use cases are governed from conception to value realization.
3.6 Awareness Operations Tower
The Awareness Operations Tower ensures that the governance workforce is trained, certified, continuously capable, and connected through a community of practice.
Key Functions:
4-Tier Training Curriculum: Progressive training from Tier 1 Fundamentals (8h) through Tier 4 Leadership (16h), totaling 64 hours of curriculum with prerequisite enforcement.
3-Level Certification Program: Career progression from Certified Data Steward (CDS) through Senior Data Steward (SDS) to Master Data Steward (MDS) with specific requirements, exams, CE hours, and work product deliverables.
Role-Based Training: Mandatory training curricula for each governance role (Owner, Business Steward, Technical Steward, Operational Steward, Project Steward, Enterprise Steward, DMO Admin, ODIAO, DPO, Security Reviewer, DQ Steward).
Certification Management: Training completion, quiz scores, exam administration, certification issuance, expiry tracking, and refresh cycles with continuing education (CE) hour tracking.
Performance Linkage: Training completion and certification level are weighted components of steward scorecards. Low training scores trigger mandatory re-training.
Awareness Campaigns: Scheduled governance awareness campaigns with tracking of participation, engagement, and knowledge retention.
Community of Practice Platform: Forums, searchable knowledge base, best practice library, case study repository, expert directory, and a 6-month guided mentorship program.
LMS Integration: Synchronization with Learning Management Systems for course catalog, enrollment, progress, and completion.
Operational Outcome: Every governance role holder is trained, certified, and current. Training gaps are identified automatically. Steward performance is directly linked to knowledge, capability, and certification level. Stewards are connected through a vibrant community of practice with mentorship, knowledge sharing, and expert access.
The six towers operate as an integrated ecosystem through DGOP. No tower is an island. Governance Operations provides the accountability foundation for Compliance Operations. Compliance Operations provides the evidence for Data Operations. Data Operations provides the quality for Transparency Operations. Transparency Operations provides the public trust for Value Operations. Value Operations provides the business case for Awareness Operations. And Awareness Operations ensures the workforce can operate all five other towers effectively.
4. Governance Structure
DGOP v4.1 does not operate in a vacuum. It requires a clearly defined governance structure with defined roles, responsibilities, and decision rights. The following governance bodies and functions are essential for the successful operation of DGOP.
4.1 Data Governance Steering Committee (DGSC)
The DGSC is the highest governance authority in the entity. It provides strategic direction, resolves escalations, and approves major governance policies and investments.
Composition:
Chair: Deputy Minister / CEO or designated executive sponsor
Members: CDO, CIO, CISO, DPO, CFO, Legal Counsel, ODIAO, DMO Head, and representatives from major business domains
Secretariat: DMO
Responsibilities:
Approve data governance strategy, policies, and standards
Approve major DGOP configuration changes (new domains, roles, assignment rules)
Resolve ownership conflicts escalated from the Data Council
Review and approve NDI readiness reports before submission to SDAIA
Approve data sharing agreements with external entities
Review and approve data asset valuation and monetization strategies
Approve the Data Governance Charter and its amendments
Fund and prioritize DGOP enhancement initiatives
Meeting Frequency: Monthly (operational) and Quarterly (strategic)
DGOP Integration: DGSC receives automated executive dashboards, exception reports, and escalation summaries from DGOP. All DGSC decisions are logged as workflow cases with evidence attachments. Charter approval workflows route through DGSC as the final authority.
4.2 Data Council
The Data Council is the operational governance body that oversees day-to-day governance activities, reviews stewardship performance, and makes tactical decisions.
Composition:
Chair: Chief Data Officer (CDO) or Head of DMO
Members: Domain owners, DMO team leads, DPO representative, Security representative, ODIAO, Enterprise Data Steward, and selected senior stewards
Secretariat: DMO
Responsibilities:
Review and approve domain-level governance policies and standards
Review stewardship assignments and resolve conflicts within domains
Monitor NDI domain readiness scores and approve remediation plans
Review DQ issue trends and approve enterprise-wide improvement initiatives
Approve Open Data candidates and FOI exemption justifications
Review steward performance scorecards and approve training interventions
Escalate unresolved issues to the DGSC
Approve new or amended Data Governance Charter elements
Meeting Frequency: Bi-weekly
DGOP Integration: Data Council meetings are scheduled and tracked in DGOP. Agenda items are linked to workflow cases, KPI trends, and evidence review queues. Meeting minutes are stored as governance evidence. Policy lifecycle workflows route through Data Council for domain-level approvals.
4.3 Working Groups
Working Groups are domain-specific or topic-specific teams that perform detailed governance work and report to the Data Council.

4.4 Data Management Office (DMO)
The DMO is the operational engine of data governance. It administers DGOP, supports stewards and owners, manages NDI compliance, and coordinates with SDAIA. The DMO is sized according to entity scale (see Section 9: DGPO Sizing Guidance).
DMO Functions:
DGOP Administration: Configure domains, subjects, capabilities, roles, rules, workflows, KPIs, and training plans.
Ownership Management: Monitor ownership gaps, resolve exceptions, manage the exception queue, and escalate conflicts.
NDI Coordination: Map operational activities to NDI specifications, manage evidence collection, coordinate with SDAIA assessors, and produce audit packs.
Steward Support: Provide training, answer questions, troubleshoot workflow issues, steward performance coaching, and community of practice facilitation.
Reporting: Produce operational reports, executive dashboards, NDI readiness reports, and compliance summaries.
Continuous Improvement: Analyze governance metrics, identify bottlenecks, propose process improvements, manage DGOP enhancement requests, and conduct 6-month maturity assessments.
DMO Structure:
DMO Head / Chief Data Officer
Governance Operations Team (ownership, stewardship, rules, workflows, charter/policy management)
Compliance Operations Team (NDI, PDPL, NCA, evidence management)
Data Operations Team (DQ, metadata, MCM, DAM, DCM, RMD)
Transparency Operations Team (OD, FOI, portal integration)
Value Operations Team (BIA, DVR, data valuation)
Awareness Operations Team (training, certification, community, communication)
DGOP Integration: The DMO operates entirely within DGOP. Every DMO function has a corresponding DGOP module, dashboard, and workflow.
4.5 Open Data & Information Access Officer (ODIAO)
The ODIAO is a specialized role responsible for the operational management of Open Data and Freedom of Information activities. This role is mandated by NDI and is critical for transparency compliance.
ODIAO Responsibilities:
Manage the Open Data candidate pipeline from identification to publication
Oversee the FOI request lifecycle from intake to closure
Ensure SLA compliance for both Open Data updates and FOI responses
Coordinate with data owners, privacy officers, and security reviewers for OD/FOI approvals
Manage the ODIAO Operating Cockpit in DGOP
Produce transparency reports for SDAIA and internal stakeholders
Handle appeals and escalations related to OD/FOI decisions
DGOP Integration: The ODIAO has a dedicated dashboard in DGOP showing real-time backlog, SLA status, risk alerts, and performance metrics. All OD/FOI workflows route through the ODIAO for review and approval.
4.6 Data Protection Officer (DPO)
The DPO is responsible for PDPL compliance and personal data protection across the entity. In DGOP, the DPO role is operationalized through embedded workflows and evidence management.
DPO Responsibilities:
Approve personal data processing activities and maintain the Record of Processing Activities (RoPA)
Review and approve Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)
Manage consent records, including capture, versioning, withdrawal, and audit
Oversee Data Subject Request (DSR) handling including access, rectification, erasure, and portability
Manage breach notification workflows with 72-hour regulatory reporting
Approve cross-border data transfer requests with Standard Contractual Clauses
Provide privacy review for Open Data candidates and FOI requests involving personal data
DGOP Integration: The DPO is assigned as Privacy Steward for all personal data subjects. DPIA, DSR, consent, and breach workflows route to the DPO for approval. PDPL evidence is stored in the NDI Compliance Hub with the DPO as evidence owner.
4.7 Information Security Function
The Information Security function ensures that data governance aligns with cybersecurity requirements under NCA ECC-1:2024.
Security Responsibilities:
Review and approve classification assignments (Restricted, Secret, Top Secret)
Oversee DLP incident review and remediation workflows
Conduct periodic access reviews and certify access rights
Approve exceptions to security policies with risk acceptance
Monitor SIEM alerts related to data governance activities
Ensure encryption, key management, and HSM compliance
Perform security assessments of DGOP infrastructure and integrations
DGOP Integration: Security reviewers are assigned automatically based on classification rules. DLP incidents create workflow cases routed to Security Stewards. Access reviews are scheduled and tracked in DGOP with automated escalation for non-completion.

9. DGPO Sizing Guidance *(NEW --- Gap Closure)*
The Data Governance Program Office (DGPO), implemented as the DMO within DGOP, requires appropriate staffing to be effective. DGOP v4.1 provides entity-sizing guidance to determine the optimal DMO size based on organizational characteristics.
9.1 Entity Sizing Model
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Size Criteria Typical Profile
------------ ---------------------------------------------------------- -----------------------------------------------------------
Small \< 500 employees OR \< 10 data domains OR \< 50 stewards Small ministry, independent agency, municipal office
Medium 500-3,000 employees OR 10-25 domains OR 50-200 stewards Medium ministry, regulatory authority, large municipality
Large \> 3,000 employees OR \> 25 domains OR \> 200 stewards Large ministry, national authority, multi-branch entity
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Sizing Calculator Formula
DGOP provides an automated sizing calculator based on the following weighted formula:
Base FTEs = 2 (minimum viable DMO)
Employee Factor: ROUND(Employee Count / 1000) * 0.5
Domain Factor: ROUND(Domain Count / 5) * 0.5
Steward Factor: ROUND(Steward Count / 25) * 0.5
System Factor: ROUND(System Count / 20) * 0.3
NDI Domain Factor: Active NDI Domains * 0.2
Size Classification:
Small: Total FTEs <= 3
Medium: Total FTEs 4-7
Large: Total FTEs >= 8
Example: An entity with 2,000 employees, 15 domains, 120 stewards, 30 systems, and 12 active NDI domains:
Base: 2.0
Employees: 2,000/1000 * 0.5 = 1.0
Domains: 15/5 * 0.5 = 1.5
Stewards: 120/25 * 0.5 = 2.4
Systems: 30/20 * 0.3 = 0.45
NDI: 12 * 0.2 = 2.4
Total: 2.0 + 1.0 + 1.5 + 2.4 + 0.45 + 2.4 = 9.75 -> Large (10 FTEs)

11. RACI Matrices
RACI (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) matrices define who does what in every governance activity. DGOP v4.1 embeds RACI into workflows so that every task is automatically assigned to the correct role based on the RACI template for that activity.
RACI Definitions:
R -- Responsible: The person who performs the work. There may be multiple Rs.
A -- Accountable: The person who owns the outcome and has decision authority. There is only one A per activity.
C -- Consulted: People who provide input and whose opinions are sought. There may be multiple Cs.
I -- Informed: People who are notified of decisions and outcomes but do not participate in the decision. There may be multiple Is.

18.3.3 Fuzzy Search

Near-Match Suggestions: When exact matches are not found, the system suggests similar terms ("Did you mean...?").

Boolean Operators: AND, OR, NOT operators for complex queries (data quality AND owner:Ahmed NOT archived).
Wildcard Matching: * for multiple characters, ? for single character (e.g., cust* matches "customer", "custom").

**Category** | **Description** | **Examples** | **User Message Approach**
**Validation Errors** | Input data does not meet field requirements | Missing required field; invalid email format; data type mismatch; field length exceeded | Identify the specific field, state the validation rule, and show the acceptable format
**Business Rule Errors** | The operation violates a configured business rule | RACI violation (unauthorized action); insufficient approval authority; SLA breach; duplicate assignment | Explain the rule that was violated, who has the required authority, and how to request an exception
**System Errors** | Technical failure in DGOP or a dependent service | Database timeout; integration service unavailable; file storage error; memory limit exceeded | Apologize for the inconvenience, explain the general nature of the issue (without technical detail), provide estimated resolution time if known, and offer a retry or support contact
**Permission Errors** | User lacks the required authorization | Attempting to edit a read-only record; accessing a restricted domain; insufficient role privilege | State the required permission clearly, identify who can grant it, and provide a request workflow link
**Workflow Errors** | Failure in workflow execution | Invalid state transition; missing approver in routing chain; circular delegation detected; workflow version conflict | Explain where in the workflow the issue occurred, show the current state, and provide the correct next step or escalation path
**Data Quality Errors** | DQ rule execution or profiling anomaly | DQ rule execution failure; profiling threshold breach; data pattern anomaly; reference data mismatch | State which DQ rule was violated, show the expected vs. actual value, and link to the remediation workflow

**Session & Security Errors** | Authentication or session-related failures | Session expired; authentication failed; IP restriction violation; concurrent session limit reached; password expired | Provide clear re-authentication instructions, explain security measures (positively), and offer support for access issues
